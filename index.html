How it works

Each point is placed by walking forward from the previous one. X/Y distance controls how far apart points can be horizontally and vertically. Rotation turns the walking direction a little at each step. Variance adds randomness to the direction — low values make straighter paths, high values make wilder ones. Noise frequency controls how smoothly that randomness changes — low is flowing, high is jerky. Margin keeps points away from the page edges. You can pin points (Shift+click) so they stay in place when you regenerate.
